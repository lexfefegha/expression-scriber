EXPRESSION-SCRIBER — HOW TO USE
================================

Your body is the controller. Move in front of your webcam
and four creative tools turn your movement into art and sound.

Everything runs in your browser. Nothing is sent anywhere.


GETTING STARTED
---------------

1. Open the app in Chrome or Edge on a laptop/desktop.
   (Mobile is not supported — you need a keyboard and a webcam.)

2. Allow camera access when your browser asks.

3. You'll see four cards on the home screen. Click one to start.

4. Stand so your full upper body (at least) is visible to the camera.
   A small webcam preview in the corner shows what the camera sees.

5. Wait a few seconds for the pose model to load (you'll see a percentage).

6. Start moving. The main canvas shows the result in real time.


THE FOUR INSTRUMENTS
--------------------

MOVEMENT SCRIPT  〰
  Your body writes. Words follow your motion and fade away.
  Two modes:
    Trail  — text follows you and fades behind like a comet tail.
             You control the trail length.
    Paint  — text stamps where you are and slowly dissolves.
             You control how fast it fades.
  Pick which body parts the text attaches to (nose, wrists,
  elbows, shoulders, etc.). Change the text, font, color, size,
  and whether words repeat or cycle one by one.

SPOKEN BODY  ◎
  Speak out loud. Your words appear on your body in real time,
  wrapping between your shoulders and around your head. The browser
  uses your microphone for speech recognition — allow mic access
  when asked. You can change text color and size.

WORN IMAGE  ❏
  Pick an emoji for each body part — head, torso, upper arms,
  forearms, thighs, shins. The emoji sticks to your limbs and
  moves when you move. Choose from a palette of 50 emoji. Each
  body part gets its own dropdown so you can mix and match.

FELT SOUND  ◍
  Your body becomes a musical instrument.
    - Raise your RIGHT hand  → pitch goes up
    - Spread your arms wide  → volume goes up
    - Raise your LEFT hand   → sound gets brighter
    - Tilt your shoulders    → detune / wobble effect
  Controls let you pick a base note (C2 through C5), waveform
  shape (sawtooth, triangle, sine, square), and reverb amount.
  Three visualization styles:
    Waveform  — a sound wave drawn across your body
    Spectrum  — frequency bars centered on your torso
    Rings     — expanding circles at your wrists and shoulders
  Click the canvas once if you don't hear sound (browsers block
  audio until you interact with the page). Use the Mute toggle
  to silence audio without leaving the sketch.


THE CONTROL PANEL
-----------------

Every instrument has a panel on the right side of the screen.
Use it to tweak colors, fonts, sizes, emojis, and behavior
without writing any code.

  ← Back     Returns to the home screen.
  ● Rec      Records a video of your canvas. Click once to start
             recording, click again to stop. The video downloads
             automatically as a .webm file.

Each instrument also has:
  Background     — change the canvas background color.
  Webcam preview — toggle the small camera feed on or off.
  Debug overlay  — show keypoint names, confidence scores,
                   and tracked-part highlights (useful for
                   understanding what the pose model sees).


TIPS
----

- Good lighting helps the camera see you better.
- Stand about 3–6 feet from the camera.
- A plain background behind you works best.
- If tracking is jumpy, try moving more slowly.
- You can resize the browser window — the canvas adapts.
- Turn on the debug overlay to see exactly which keypoints
  are being tracked and how confident the model is.
